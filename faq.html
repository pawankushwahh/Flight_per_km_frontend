General Questions

What is the Flight Cost Intelligence System?

The Flight Cost Intelligence System is a comprehensive tool that helps travelers make smarter flight decisions by analyzing the cost per kilometer of flights. By breaking down flight costs to a per-kilometer basis, we provide a standardized way to compare flight values across different routes, airlines, and travel classes.

Why is cost per kilometer a better metric than total price?

Total price alone doesn't tell you if you're getting good value for your money. A $500 flight might seem expensive, but if it's covering 5,000 kilometers, that's only $0.10 per kilometer, which is quite reasonable. Conversely, a $200 flight covering just 500 kilometers costs $0.40 per kilometer, which is relatively expensive.

Cost per kilometer allows you to standardize flight prices across different distances, making it easier to compare value and spot good deals regardless of the route length.

Methodology

How do you calculate the distance between airports?

We use the Haversine formula to calculate the great-circle distance between airports based on their latitude and longitude coordinates. This gives us the shortest distance between two points on a sphere (Earth), which is a good approximation of the actual flight distance.

It's worth noting that actual flight paths may be longer due to air traffic control routing, weather conditions, and other factors. However, the great-circle distance provides a consistent basis for comparison.

How do you determine if a nearby airport is worth considering?

We analyze the average cost per kilometer difference between flights from the primary airport and flights from nearby alternatives. If a nearby airport offers at least a 5% reduction in cost per kilometer, we consider it worth recommending.

We also factor in the distance to the alternative airport. An airport that's 150km away might offer cheaper flights, but the ground transportation costs and time could negate those savings. Our system aims to provide a holistic view of these trade-offs.

How do you calculate the value rating for travel classes?

Our value rating for travel classes is based on the relationship between the price increase and the space/amenities increase when upgrading from economy class. For example, if business class costs 3 times more than economy but offers only 2 times more space and amenities, it would receive a lower value rating.

We use a 5-star rating system where 5 stars indicates exceptional value (the benefits outweigh the cost increase) and 1 star indicates poor value (the cost increase significantly outweighs the benefits).

Features

What is the Dynamic Pricing Predictor?

The Dynamic Pricing Predictor analyzes historical pricing trends for specific routes to identify patterns in how flight costs per kilometer change throughout the year and as the departure date approaches.

It helps you determine the best time to book your flight (e.g., 30-60 days before departure) and the best time of year to travel (e.g., November) to get the lowest cost per kilometer.

How does the Layover Value Analyzer work?

The Layover Value Analyzer compares direct flights with flights that include layovers to determine if the cost savings justify the additional travel time. It calculates the cost per kilometer for both options and shows you how much money you could save by accepting a layover, as well as the time trade-off.

This helps you make an informed decision based on your priorities: if saving money is more important than saving time, a layover might be worth it; if time is more valuable to you, a direct flight might be the better choice despite the higher cost.

What does the Global Cost-per-Km Heat Map show?

The Global Cost-per-Km Heat Map visualizes average flight costs per kilometer across different regions and countries around the world. It helps you identify which parts of the world have relatively expensive or inexpensive air travel.

This can be particularly useful for planning multi-destination trips or for understanding global aviation pricing trends. For example, you might discover that flights within Southeast Asia are generally much cheaper per kilometer than flights within Europe.

Technical

What technologies are used to build this system?

The Flight Cost Intelligence System is built using:

  • Frontend: HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript (no frameworks)
  • Backend: Python with Flask
  • Data Storage: SQL database for storing flight data and user preferences
  • Visualization: Chart.js for graphs and Leaflet for maps
  • APIs: Integration with real flight data providers

This technology stack was chosen for its simplicity, performance, and ease of deployment.
